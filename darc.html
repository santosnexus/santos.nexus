Animal Welfare, Biodiversity & Habitat Conservation

DARC Foundation

Conservation begins where we live. We restore biodiversity through native fruit tree forests, protect natural habitats, and support domestic animal welfare in Kerala — creating spaces where people, animals, birds, insects, reptiles, and native wildlife can thrive together.

Donate or Partner Our Work
What We Do

Three programmes, one mission.

DARC Foundation works across three interconnected programmes — because biodiversity, animal welfare, and habitat conservation are not separable.

🌳

Biodiversity Restoration

Native fruit tree forests, planted and maintained by DARC volunteers and partner farmers. Bringing vanishing indigenous species back to the Kerala landscape.

🐾

Domestic Animal Welfare

Rescue, rehabilitation, and rehoming of injured and abandoned domestic animals. With a network of partner vets and foster homes across Kerala.

🌿

Habitat Conservation

Protecting the natural habitats that hold biodiversity together — wetlands, mangroves, forest edges, and the corridors between them.

🏫

School Education

In-school and field-trip programmes for Kerala schools. Because the conservationists of 2050 are in 5th grade today.

🤝

Volunteer Engagement

Weekend planting drives, beach clean-ups, animal shelter support. The community is the work.

📊

Research & Reporting

Annual biodiversity reports, species counts, and impact assessments. Transparent, with data — because conservation without measurement is just gardening.
